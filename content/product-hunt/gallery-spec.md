# Product Hunt — Gallery & Screenshots Spec

Five screenshots for the PH gallery. Each should be exported at 1280×800 (PH standard) from the live product or a polished mockup.

---

## Screenshot 1 — Hero / What It Does

**Show:** The landing page hero section with the headline "Find subscriptions you forgot you had", the subheadline, and the two CTAs ("Connect Your Bank" and "Upload CSV").

**Annotation (small overlay text, bottom-left):** Connect your bank or upload a CSV — 30 seconds.

**Purpose:** Communicate the product instantly. A visitor scrolling through PH should understand what SubSnoop does within 3 seconds of seeing this image.

---

## Screenshot 2 — Detection Results

**Show:** The dashboard subscription list view with 8-10 detected subscriptions. Include a mix of well-known brands (Netflix, Spotify, Adobe) and one or two "surprise" detections (e.g., a forgotten cloud service or old gym membership). Show the monthly cost next to each and the total monthly/annual spend prominently at the top.

**Annotation:** "€127.43/month across 9 subscriptions. 3 marked as unused."

**Purpose:** Demonstrate the core value — finding subscriptions people didn't know they had. The total cost number should be high enough to create a "wait, I spend that much?" reaction.

---

## Screenshot 3 — Subscription Detail / Cancel Flow

**Show:** A single subscription detail card expanded. Show the merchant name, amount, billing frequency, "Next billing: June 15", and a prominent red "Cancel Subscription" button. Below the button, show a short text: "We'll take you to Spotify's cancellation page — takes 30 seconds."

**Annotation:** One click to cancel. No phone calls, no chatbots.

**Purpose:** Show how easy it is to cancel. Address the #1 user anxiety: "finding it is great, but can I actually cancel it?"

---

## Screenshot 4 — Security / Trust

**Show:** A dedicated security or onboarding screen showing the PSD2 connection flow. Include: a bank selection dropdown (Swedbank, SEB, LHV, Luminor, Coop Pank), a lock icon, and three trust badges: "Read-only access", "Bank-grade encryption", "GDPR compliant", "Registered AISP".

**Annotation:** We can see your transactions. We can never move your money.

**Purpose:** Address the #1 objection — "is this safe?" — before the visitor even asks it. Visual proof of security builds trust faster than text.

---

## Screenshot 5 — Pricing / Upgrade

**Show:** The three-tier pricing table (Free / Pro €5.99 / Premium €9.99) with the most popular tier highlighted. Include a line at the top: "Free forever for up to 5 subscriptions" and at the bottom: "Cancel your Pro subscription anytime — just like the ones SubSnoop finds."

**Annotation:** Free forever. Upgrade only when you need more.

**Purpose:** Remove pricing anxiety. Show that the free tier is genuinely useful (not a crippled demo) and that Pro is cheap relative to the savings found.

---

## Production Notes

- Use real UI, not mockups. Screenshots from actual product build more trust than Figma designs.
- Blur or use test data for any personally identifiable information in screenshots.
- Export at 1280×800 PNG. File names: `01-hero.png`, `02-detection.png`, `03-cancel.png`, `04-security.png`, `05-pricing.png`.
- Optional 6th asset: a 30-second GIF or short video showing the connect → scan → results flow end-to-end.
