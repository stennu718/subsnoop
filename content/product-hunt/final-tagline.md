# Final Tagline — SubSnoop on Product Hunt

Sixty characters is the hard ceiling. Below are the main tagline and two A/B-test candidates. All three emphasise a **single strong outcome** (finding forgot subs), vary in tone, and stay within budget.

---

## Main tagline (used on PH listing)

> **Find and cancel forgotten subscriptions in 30 seconds**

_48 characters. Leads with the outcome, has a credibility signal (30 seconds), matches hero copy on subsnoop.ee landing._

---

## Variation A — outcome + bank context

> **Connect your bank. We'll find your forgotten subscriptions.**

_57 characters. Slightly longer, emphasises the bank-connect mechanism. Good for users who wonder "how?"_

---

## Variation B — emotional / curiosity gap

> **The subscriptions your bank won't tell you about.**

_49 characters. Different angle — positions the product as an insider revealing a hidden layer. Good for retargeting ads and the Twitter launch post._

---

## Usage note

- **PH listing tagline field**: use the main tagline (48 chars).
- **PH first comment opening**: open with the main tagline in **bold**, then expand.
- **Twitter**: Variation C works best for the main launch tweet because it's curiosity-driven.
- **Landing hero headline**: keep "Find subscriptions you forgot you had" on the site — don't overwrite it with the PH tagline. They coexist.
- **Retargeting ads**: test A vs. C. Skip B for ads (too similar to A).
