# Data Processing Agreement — Stripe

**SubSnoop OÜ × Stripe Payments Europe, Ltd**

*Effective date: 28 June 2026*

*Version: 1.0*

---

## 1. Parties

| Role | Entity |
|---|---|
| **Data Controller** | SubSnoop OÜ, Estonia |
| **Data Processor** | Stripe Payments Europe, Ltd (a Stripe Inc. subsidiary), incorporated in Ireland |

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") supplements the Stripe Services Agreement and is made pursuant to **Article 28(3) GDPR**.

---

## 2. Scope and Purpose of Processing

Stripe processes personal data on behalf of SubSnoop for the following purposes:

- Processing subscription payments (Pro and Premium plans).
- Managing recurring billing and invoicing.
- Fraud detection and prevention (Stripe Radar).
- Generating payment receipts and transaction records.
- Handling refunds and chargebacks.

---

## 3. Categories of Personal Data

Stripe processes the following categories of personal data on behalf of SubSnoop:

| Data Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Identity data | Name, email address |
| Payment data | Card number (tokenised), bank account (tokenised), billing address |
| Transaction data | Amount, currency, timestamp, transaction status |
| Technical data | IP address, device fingerprint (for fraud prevention) |

**Note:** Stripe does not process bank transaction data retrieved via PSD2 APIs. That data is handled exclusively by GoCardless/Nordigen.

---

## 4. Data Subject Categories

- SubSnoop users who subscribe to paid plans (Pro / Premium).
- EU/EEA consumers and businesses.

---

## 5. Obligations of Stripe (Processor)

In accordance with Article 28(3) GDPR, Stripe shall:

1. **Process personal data only on documented instructions** from SubSnoop, including with regard to transfers of personal data to a third country or international organisation.
2. **Ensure confidentiality** — persons authorised to process personal data have committed themselves to confidentiality.
3. **Implement appropriate security measures** in accordance with Article 32 GDPR, including encryption, ability to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems.
4. **Not engage another processor** (sub-processor) without prior specific or general written authorisation of SubSnoop. In the case of general written authorisation, Stripe shall inform SubSnoop of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of sub-processors.
5. **Assist SubSnoop** in fulfilling data subject rights requests (Art. 15–22 GDPR).
6. **Assist SubSnoop** in ensuring compliance with obligations pursuant to Articles 32–36 GDPR (security, breach notification, DPIA, prior consultation).
7. **Delete or return all personal data** to SubSnoop after the end of the provision of services, and delete existing copies unless EU or Member State law requires storage.
8. **Make available to SubSnoop** all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with Article 28 GDPR and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections.

---

## 6. Sub-Processors

Stripe may engage sub-processors as listed in the [Stripe Sub-Processor List](https://stripe.com/legal/sub-processors). Stripe shall:
- Maintain an up-to-date list of sub-processors.
- Notify SubSnoop of any new sub-processors at least 30 days in advance.
- Allow SubSnoop to object to new sub-processors.

---

## 7. International Data Transfers

Stripe Payments Europe, Ltd processes personal data within the **EU/EEA**. Where Stripe Inc. (US parent) requires access to data, transfers are safeguarded by:
- **EU Standard Contractual Clauses** (Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914).
- **EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework** (where applicable and certified).
- Supplementary measures including encryption and access controls.

---

## 8. Data Retention

Stripe retains personal data as necessary for:
- **Payment processing and fraud prevention:** Up to 7 years (legal obligation under anti-money laundering directives).
- **Accounting records:** 7 years (Estonian Accounting Act, § 37).
- Upon termination of the Stripe Services Agreement, Stripe shall delete or return personal data in accordance with its data retention policies and this DPA.

---

## 9. Security Measures

Stripe maintains industry-leading security measures, including:
- **PCI DSS Level 1 compliance** (highest level).
- **AES-256 encryption** for data at rest.
- **TLS 1.2+** for data in transit.
- **SOC 2 Type II** certification.
- **ISO 27001** certification.
- Regular penetration testing and vulnerability assessments.

---

## 10. Data Breach Notification

Stripe shall notify SubSnoop without undue delay (and in any event within **72 hours**) after becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting SubSnoop data, in accordance with Article 33(2) GDPR.

---

## 11. Audit Rights

SubSnoop may audit Stripe's compliance with this DPA:
- Upon **30 days' written notice**.
- No more than **once per year** (unless a breach or regulatory requirement necessitates additional audits).
- Stripe may provide SOC 2 / ISO 27001 reports in lieu of on-site audits.
